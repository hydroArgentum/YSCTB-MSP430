Part 1: Some Initial Measurements

November 30, 2017

First, let’s do some initial measurements so we know what to expect when designing the project.

I’ve decided to do this by measuring what the lighting in my living area can provide. To measure this out, I’ll be using the same device that I’ll be using to actually detect the amount of light in the project, a photoresistor.

So, I’ve laid out the photoresistor on a breadboard so that I can measure the resistance at the current light level.

Looking at the resulting reading, we can see that the resistor at the ambient lighting is roughly 53.3 kΩ.

Since we want to lean towards conserving electricity and the fact that the circuit will probably be a little further away from the light, we can assume that our desired threshold resistance should be around 60 kΩ.
